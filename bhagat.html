Bhagat Singh (1907–1931)

Bhagat Singh was one of the most famous Indian freedom fighters. He became a symbol of courage and patriotism for his powerful role in India’s struggle for independence from British rule.

Early Life

Bhagat Singh was born on 28 September 1907 in Banga village (now in Pakistan). He belonged to a patriotic Sikh family. From childhood, he was inspired by the freedom movement and dreamed of an independent India.

Contribution to Freedom Struggle

Bhagat Singh joined revolutionary groups to fight against British oppression. He became a member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA). He strongly believed that India needed freedom and justice for all.

Key Actions

Bhagat Singh is best known for taking bold steps against British rule. He and his companions carried out protests and revolutionary activities to awaken Indians against British injustice.

Lala Lajpat Rai Case

In 1928, the great leader Lala Lajpat Rai died after being beaten during a protest. Bhagat Singh and his friends decided to take revenge. They planned to punish the responsible officer but mistakenly killed John Saunders instead of James Scott.

Assembly Bomb Incident

In 1929, Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt threw two non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly (Delhi) to protest against British laws. They did not want to kill anyone. They shouted “Inquilab Zindabad” and willingly got arrested to spread their message.

Martyrdom

Bhagat Singh was sentenced to death. On 23 March 1931, he was hanged along with Rajguru and Sukhdev. He became a martyr and an immortal hero of India.

Why He Is Remembered

Bhagat Singh is remembered for his fearless spirit, revolutionary ideas, and sacrifice for India’s freedom. He is one of the greatest inspirations for Indian youth even today.
