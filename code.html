<h1>Rao Tula Ram (1825–1863)</h1>

<p><b>Rao Tula Ram</b> was a famous Indian freedom fighter and ruler from <b>Rewari (Haryana)</b>. He played an important role in the <b>Revolt of 1857</b> against British rule.</p>

<h2>Early Life</h2>
<p>Rao Tula Ram was born in <b>1825</b> in the Rewari region. He became the Rao (chief/ruler) of Rewari and was known for his bravery and leadership.</p>

<h2>Role in the Revolt of 1857</h2>
<p>In <b>1857</b>, Rao Tula Ram raised an army and fought against the British. He supported freedom fighters and tried to unite local rulers to oppose British control.</p>

<h2>Struggle and Efforts</h2>
<p>After strong British attacks, he moved towards <b>Rajasthan and Punjab</b> to seek support. Later, he also went abroad (towards <b>Afghanistan/Iran</b>) to request help for India’s freedom struggle.</p>

<h2>Death</h2>
<p>Rao Tula Ram died in <b>1863</b>. He is remembered as one of the greatest heroes of the 1857 revolt, especially in <b>Haryana</b>.</p>

<h2>Why He Is Remembered</h2>
<p>Rao Tula Ram is respected for his courage and contributions to India’s fight for freedom. He is considered one of the biggest revolutionaries of Haryana during the 1857 uprising.</p>

<h1>Rani Lakshmibai (1828–1858)</h1>

<p><b>Rani Lakshmibai</b>, also known as the <b>Rani of Jhansi</b>, was one of the greatest freedom fighters of India. She played a leading role in the <b>Revolt of 1857</b> against British rule and is remembered for her bravery and patriotism.</p>

<h2>Early Life</h2>
<p>Rani Lakshmibai was born in <b>1828</b> in <b>Varanasi</b>. Her childhood name was <b>Manikarnika Tambe</b> and she was lovingly called <b>Manu</b>. From a young age, she was trained in <b>horse riding, sword fighting, and archery</b>.</p>

<h2>Marriage and Becoming Queen</h2>
<p>She married <b>Maharaja Gangadhar Rao</b>, the ruler of Jhansi, and became the <b>Queen of Jhansi</b>. After the death of her husband, she adopted a son named <b>Damodar Rao</b>.</p>

<h2>Reason Behind the Revolt</h2>
<p>The British tried to take over Jhansi using the <b>Doctrine of Lapse</b>, saying that Jhansi would not be given to the adopted child. Rani Lakshmibai strongly opposed this and declared that Jhansi was her rightful kingdom.</p>

<h2>Role in the Revolt of 1857</h2>
<p>In <b>1857</b>, she became one of the main leaders of the rebellion. She defended Jhansi bravely and fought against the British army with great courage.</p>

<h2>Battle and Martyrdom</h2>
<p>When the British attacked Jhansi, Rani Lakshmibai fought fiercely. She later joined other freedom fighters and fought in <b>Gwalior</b>. On <b>18 June 1858</b>, she was martyred while fighting the British. She became a symbol of bravery and sacrifice.</p>

<h2>Why She Is Remembered</h2>
<p>Rani Lakshmibai is remembered as the <b>“Queen of Courage”</b>. She inspired millions of Indians to fight for freedom and is one of the most respected heroes of India’s independence struggle.</p>

<h1>Bhagat Singh (1907–1931)</h1>

<p><b>Bhagat Singh</b> was one of the most famous Indian freedom fighters. He became a symbol of courage and patriotism for his powerful role in India’s struggle for independence from British rule.</p>

<h2>Early Life</h2>
<p>Bhagat Singh was born on <b>28 September 1907</b> in <b>Banga village</b> (now in Pakistan). He belonged to a patriotic Sikh family. From childhood, he was inspired by the freedom movement and dreamed of an independent India.</p>

<h2>Contribution to Freedom Struggle</h2>
<p>Bhagat Singh joined revolutionary groups to fight against British oppression. He became a member of the <b>Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA)</b>. He strongly believed that India needed freedom and justice for all.</p>

<h2>Key Actions</h2>
<p>Bhagat Singh is best known for taking bold steps against British rule. He and his companions carried out protests and revolutionary activities to awaken Indians against British injustice.</p>

<h2>Lala Lajpat Rai Case</h2>
<p>In <b>1928</b>, the great leader <b>Lala Lajpat Rai</b> died after being beaten during a protest. Bhagat Singh and his friends decided to take revenge. They planned to punish the responsible officer but mistakenly killed <b>John Saunders</b> instead of James Scott.</p>

<h2>Assembly Bomb Incident</h2>
<p>In <b>1929</b>, Bhagat Singh and <b>Batukeshwar Dutt</b> threw two non-lethal bombs in the <b>Central Legislative Assembly (Delhi)</b> to protest against British laws. They did not want to kill anyone. They shouted <b>“Inquilab Zindabad”</b> and willingly got arrested to spread their message.</p>

<h2>Martyrdom</h2>
<p>Bhagat Singh was sentenced to death. On <b>23 March 1931</b>, he was hanged along with <b>Rajguru</b> and <b>Sukhdev</b>. He became a martyr and an immortal hero of India.</p>

<h2>Why He Is Remembered</h2>
<p>Bhagat Singh is remembered for his fearless spirit, revolutionary ideas, and sacrifice for India’s freedom. He is one of the greatest inspirations for Indian youth even today.</p>
