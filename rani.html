Rani Lakshmibai (1828–1858)

Rani Lakshmibai, also known as the Rani of Jhansi, was one of the greatest freedom fighters of India. She played a leading role in the Revolt of 1857 against British rule and is remembered for her bravery and patriotism.

Early Life

Rani Lakshmibai was born in 1828 in Varanasi. Her childhood name was Manikarnika Tambe and she was lovingly called Manu. From a young age, she was trained in horse riding, sword fighting, and archery.

Marriage and Becoming Queen

She married Maharaja Gangadhar Rao, the ruler of Jhansi, and became the Queen of Jhansi. After the death of her husband, she adopted a son named Damodar Rao.

Reason Behind the Revolt

The British tried to take over Jhansi using the Doctrine of Lapse, saying that Jhansi would not be given to the adopted child. Rani Lakshmibai strongly opposed this and declared that Jhansi was her rightful kingdom.

Role in the Revolt of 1857

In 1857, she became one of the main leaders of the rebellion. She defended Jhansi bravely and fought against the British army with great courage.

Battle and Martyrdom

When the British attacked Jhansi, Rani Lakshmibai fought fiercely. She later joined other freedom fighters and fought in Gwalior. On 18 June 1858, she was martyred while fighting the British. She became a symbol of bravery and sacrifice.

Why She Is Remembered

Rani Lakshmibai is remembered as the “Queen of Courage”. She inspired millions of Indians to fight for freedom and is one of the most respected heroes of India’s independence struggle.
