Rao Tula Ram (1825–1863)

Rao Tula Ram was a famous Indian freedom fighter and ruler from Rewari (Haryana). He played an important role in the Revolt of 1857 against British rule.

Early Life

Rao Tula Ram was born in 1825 in the Rewari region. He became the Rao (chief/ruler) of Rewari and was known for his bravery and leadership.

Role in the Revolt of 1857

In 1857, Rao Tula Ram raised an army and fought against the British. He supported freedom fighters and tried to unite local rulers to oppose British control.

Struggle and Efforts

After strong British attacks, he moved towards Rajasthan and Punjab to seek support. Later, he also went abroad (towards Afghanistan/Iran) to request help for India’s freedom struggle.

Death

Rao Tula Ram died in 1863. He is remembered as one of the greatest heroes of the 1857 revolt, especially in Haryana.

Why He Is Remembered

Rao Tula Ram is respected for his courage and contributions to India’s fight for freedom. He is considered one of the biggest revolutionaries of Haryana during the 1857 uprising.
